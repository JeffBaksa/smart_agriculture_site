Crop: Apple Tree

Disease: Black Rot


Cause(s) of Disease:


1. Black rot is caused by the fungus Diplodia seriata (syn Botryosphaeria obtusa).The fungus can infect dead tissue as well as living trunks, branches, leaves and fruits.
2. In wet weather, spores are released from these infections and spread by wind or splashing water. The fungus infects leaves and fruit through natural openings or minor wounds.

How to Prevent & Cure the Disease:



1. Prune out dead or diseased branches.
2. Remove infected plant material from the area.
3. Be sure to remove the stumps of any apple trees you cut down. Dead stumps can be a source of spores.
